# McEasy — C-Level Executive Profiles

Used by the C-Level Shield agent and DoE approval checklists.

---

## CEO — Raymond Sutjiono
**Focus:** Business narrative and market position

Asks about: Does this move the company forward? Is this the right bet? What story do we tell customers and investors? Will this help us win deals or retain customers?

Key question areas:
1. Vision — what's the end goal? How does it create impact in McEasy?
2. Is the value proposition specific and clear?
3. Market size — how big of revenue / engagement are we expecting in the coming year?
4. IoT / DoE: Is the definition clear? Is the logic understood? Are requirements clear?

---

## COO — Hendrik Ekowaluyo
**Focus:** Operational readiness and execution risk

Asks about: Can we actually deliver this? Do we have the people and processes? What breaks operationally if this goes wrong? How does this affect customer success and support teams?

Key question areas:
1. For IoT products: who is the vendor? Is it legitimate? How many deployments does it have? Have we done a background check?

---

## CFO — Irwan
**Focus:** Financial viability and ROI

Asks about: What does this cost to build and maintain? What is the revenue impact? What's the payback period? Are we investing in the right place given our runway?

---

## CTO — Dave Gunadi
**Focus:** Technical soundness and infrastructure

Asks about: Is the architecture right? Does this scale? What are the security implications (ISO 27001)? Are there hidden technical risks? Does this create technical debt? AWS cost impact?

---

## CBO — Andi
**Focus:** Commercial and customer acquisition

Asks about: Does this help us close deals? Is this on the customer roadmap? How does this differentiate us from competitors? Will this help with demos and sales pitches?

Key question areas:
1. What does the feel of the product look like? What's the UX flow? Is all information shown in MEP useful and coherent?
2. How does the feature differ from existing ones? How will commercial sell it to clients?
3. On release: is the costing clear and has the price been agreed? Are WI / SOPs clear?

---

## CDSO — Grady Kusmulyadi
**Focus:** Data strategy, roadmap, and competitive positioning

Asks about: Does this feature generate valuable data or consume it? Does it strengthen McEasy's data moat? Is the data architecture aligned with the long-term data strategy? Does this create a defensible competitive advantage through data network effects?

Key question areas:
1. In the bigger picture, how does this puzzle piece fit into the overall growth of the product line?
2. Can this product collaborate with other products to create new value for the customer?
3. Have you ensured the right stakeholders are approved, aware, and trained?
4. Is the timeline and expectation for different departments clear?
